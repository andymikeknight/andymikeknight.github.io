ENEL 582: Modelling and Control of Electric Machines and Drives

This set of pages forms the basis of a full set of class notes for ENEL 582 at the University of Calgary, as it is taught in Winter 2026. The course begins with an assumption of understanding AC circuits, as taught in an introductory circuits course. It does not have any Power Engineering course prerequisites, however familiarity with introductory power engineering is a benefit. The course is at a 4th year level, so familiarity with fundamental concepts of electromagnetics, Fourier analysis, and introductory controls are assumed.

The course briefly recaps important aspects of AC circuits, AC power, and electromagnetics, before introducing per-phase steady state analysis of AC induction motors. Two-axis theory of AC machines and power quantities is introduced to allow the analysis of indction motors in transient conditions, and the principles of vector control of induction motors.

Modern synchronous machine drives used in electric vehicles and high performance drive systems are covered, including surface and interior permanent magnet motors, synchronous reluctance motors, and PM assisted SynRel.

The course finishes returning to conventional fixed frequency machines with per-phase analysis of synchronous generators and steay state analysis of single phase induction motors
