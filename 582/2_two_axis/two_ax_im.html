Induction Motor qd0 Model

A three phase induction machine can be represented by three windings on the stator plus three more windings on the rotor. This representation based on a three phase wound rotor machine is also valid for a cage machine and can be seen in Fig. 1. Note that in the definition of the windings shown in the image, currents are defined as flowing into both windings from external voltage sources. In this analysis we assume that all variables are referred to the stator side via an effective turns ratio.

Each winding has a set of $abc$ voltages, currents and fluxes that we can project on to a single $qd$ reference frame. However, because the two windings are at different physical locations, and the rotor widings can move with the rotor, we will need two seperate transformation matrices, one for each of the rotor and stator.

\[ \begin{equation} K_s=\frac{2}{3}\begin{bmatrix} \cos\left(\theta\right) & \cos\left(\theta-\frac{2\pi}{3}\right) & \cos\left(\theta+\frac{2\pi}{3}\right) \\ \sin\left(\theta\right) & \sin\left(\theta-\frac{2\pi}{3}\right) & \sin\left(\theta+\frac{2\pi}{3}\right) \\ \frac{1}{2} &\frac{1}{2} &\frac{1}{2}\end{bmatrix} \end{equation} \]
\[ \begin{equation} K_r=\frac{2}{3}\begin{bmatrix} \cos\left(\theta-\theta_r \right) & \cos\left(\theta-\theta_r-\frac{2\pi}{3}\right) & \cos\left(\theta-\theta_r+\frac{2\pi}{3}\right) \\ \sin\left(\theta-\theta_r\right) & \sin\left(\theta-\theta_r-\frac{2\pi}{3}\right) & \sin\left(\theta-\theta_r+\frac{2\pi}{3}\right)\\ \frac{1}{2} &\frac{1}{2} &\frac{1}{2}\end{bmatrix} \end{equation} \]

The voltage equations for each of the stator and rotor as functions of flux linkage and current can be written as follows (negelecting zero sequence components in this course). In the analysis of induction motors in transient, we will use subscript "s" for stator variables and ubscript "r" for rotor variables.

\[ \begin{align*} v_{qds}&=R_s i_{qds} + \omega \lambda_{dqs} +\frac{d\lambda_{qds}}{dt} \\ v_{qdr}&=R_r i_{qdr} + \omega \lambda_{dqr} +\frac{d\lambda_{qdr}}{dt} \end{align*} \]

where

\[ \begin{align*} \lambda_{qds}=\begin{bmatrix}\lambda_{qs}\\ \lambda_{ds}\end{bmatrix} \qquad &\lambda_{dqs}=\begin{bmatrix}\lambda_{ds}\\ -\lambda_{qs}\end{bmatrix} \\ \lambda_{qdr}=\begin{bmatrix}\lambda_{qr}\\ \lambda_{dr}\end{bmatrix} \qquad &\lambda_{dqr}=\begin{bmatrix}\lambda_{dr}\\ -\lambda_{qr}\end{bmatrix} \end{align*} \]

The $abc$ flux linkage equations include mutual coupling within each of the stator and rotor windings, and position dependent cross coupling between the stator and rotor. The derivation of the transformation to $qd0$ is relatively complex, but the result is relatively simple and can be written as

\[ \begin{equation} \begin{bmatrix}\lambda_{qs} \\ \lambda_{ds} \\ \lambda_{qr} \\ \lambda_{dr} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} L_s & 0 &L_m & 0 \\ 0 & L_s &0 &L_m \\ L_m &0 &L_r &0 \\ 0 &L_m &0& L_r\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}i_{qs} \\ i_{ds} \\ i_{qr} \\ i_{dr} \end{bmatrix} \end{equation} \]

where $L_s = L_m+L_{ls}$, is the stator leakage inductance plus the magnetizing inductance and $L_r = L_m+L_{lr}$ is the rotor leakage inductance plus the magnetizing inductance.
